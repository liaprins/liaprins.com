Caption: The study concluded that simply starting with a couple of extra ideas meant that a would-be problem solver's mind remained more open to other ideas and inspiration.

Alt: "If your mind starts with multiple ideas in parallel, it is not prematurely committed to one path and stays open and able to receive and conceive more novel innovations." Quote taken from "Designing your life" by Bill Burnett and Dave Evans, summarizing a study conducted by the Stanford Graduate School of Education.
