Opening the wet lab to a long list of genes suspected to be connected to a disease is expensive. One way to filter down the collection is to compare it to known genesâ€”those with relationships to the disease that the research team is already aware of. Predictive Analytics (PA) is one of several tools available within the Watson for Drug Discovery (WDD) offering, and it aims to provide just that capability by searching for similarities between the way candidate and known entities 2 appear in publications. The way its results and the evidence that back them were originally conveyed, however, often left users more baffled about what to do next than they were before they opened the app. I was brought onto the WDD team to redesign the way this data and its insights were visually communicated.

At a high level, a biomedical researcher can query WDD as to which of their hypothesized gene targets are semantically similar to gene targets already known to be related to their disease of interest. Furthermore, a hierarchy fundamentally failed to communicate the data accurately.
